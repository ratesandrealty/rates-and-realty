FAQ

Common Questions

What makes a loan "jumbo"?

Any loan exceeding the FHFA conforming loan limit is a jumbo loan. For 2025, that limit is $806,500 for most counties ($1,209,750 in high-cost areas). Jumbo loans are held by the lender or sold to private investors rather than Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac.

Are jumbo rates higher than conventional?

Not necessarily. In recent years, well-qualified jumbo borrowers (700+ credit, 20%+ down, strong reserves) have received rates equal to or below conforming rates. The rate depends heavily on your credit profile, loan-to-value, and reserve strength.

What is asset depletion income?

Asset depletion allows borrowers with significant liquid assets (investments, retirement accounts) to convert them into qualifying income. Typically, the total asset value is divided by the loan term (e.g., 360 months for a 30-year loan) to arrive at a monthly income figure. Ideal for retirees or borrowers with large portfolios.

Can I get a jumbo loan for a second home or investment property?

Yes. Jumbo loans are available for primary residences, second homes, and investment properties. Investment property jumbos require larger down payments (20–30%) and slightly higher rates than primary residence jumbos.
