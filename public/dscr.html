FAQ

Common Questions

What does DSCR stand for?

Debt Service Coverage Ratio — it measures whether the rental income covers the mortgage payment. A DSCR of 1.0 means break-even; above 1.0 means the property cash-flows positive.

Do I need to show personal income for a DSCR loan?

No — that's the key advantage. We qualify the loan based on the property's rental income, not your W-2s or tax returns. Ideal for investors with complex income or multiple properties.

What's the minimum DSCR to qualify?

Most lenders require a DSCR of 0.75 or higher, though 1.0+ gets you the best rates. Some programs allow DSCR as low as 0.0 for strong borrowers.

Can I use projected rent or actual rent?

Both options exist. Some lenders use the appraiser's market rent schedule; others use an active lease. We'll find the lender whose approach works best for your property.

How many DSCR properties can I own?

DSCR lenders typically allow 10–20+ financed properties. No Fannie/Freddie limits apply. Great for scaling a portfolio.
