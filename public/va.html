FAQ

Common Questions

What is the VA funding fee?

The VA funding fee is a one-time fee paid to the VA ranging from 1.25%–3.3% of the loan amount, depending on down payment and whether it's your first use. It can be rolled into the loan. Veterans with service-connected disabilities are exempt from the funding fee.

Can I use my VA benefit more than once?

Yes. VA entitlement can be restored after your prior VA loan is paid off or when you sell the home. You can also have two simultaneous VA loans if you have remaining entitlement. There is no limit on how many times you can use the benefit over your lifetime.

Is there a VA loan limit?

Veterans with full entitlement (no active VA loans) have no loan limit. Veterans with partial entitlement (existing VA loan) are subject to county loan limits. For 2025, most counties allow up to $806,500 with zero down using full entitlement.

How do I get my Certificate of Eligibility (COE)?

We can typically obtain your COE directly through the VA's automated system in minutes. Alternatively, you can apply through VA.gov, by mail, or through your lender. You'll need your DD-214 (discharge papers) or current military ID depending on your service status.
